## PRODUCTION NOTES

**THE EMOTIONAL ARC:**

This VSL is structured like a therapy session, not a product demo.

1. **Recognition** (0:00-3:00) — Make them FEEL the experience. Sweaty hands. 3 AM phone checks. The leg bounce. The stomach drop. They need to think "this person is describing my exact life." This section should feel almost uncomfortably personal.

2. **Misdiagnosis** (3:00-4:30) — Call out the lie they've been sold. "It's your psychology" is the trading industry's favorite deflection because it shifts blame to the trader and creates infinite demand for courses, coaching, and mentorship. We're calling bullshit.

3. **Real diagnosis** (4:30-6:30) — Clinical. Specific. "You enter in the middle. Your stop is arbitrary. Your target is a guess." This should feel like a doctor finally explaining what's been wrong after years of wrong prescriptions.

4. **The cure** (6:30-11:30) — PulseWave. But we don't lead with features. We lead with "would your hands be sweating?" The product reveal is an emotional moment, not a technical demo.

5. **Redemption** (11:30-13:15) — "You're not broken." This is the moment they decide to buy. Not because of features. Because someone finally told them the truth and offered a real solution.

**Voice direction:** 

The first 3 minutes should sound like someone telling a story they've lived. Quiet. Intimate. Like you're talking to one person in a room, not performing for a camera.

The "diagnosis" section should shift to slightly more authoritative — still warm, but now you're the person who figured it out and you're explaining what you found.

The product section can have slightly more energy — you're showing them something that excites you. But never hype. Never "BUT WAIT." Think: a friend showing you something cool, not an infomercial.

The reframe ("you're not broken") should be the most emotionally raw moment in the entire video. Slow down. Drop the music out. Make eye contact with the camera (or in audio-only, let the silence carry weight). This is where they feel seen.

**KEY MOMENTS to nail in recording:**

1. The 3 AM phone check (0:15-3:00) — if this doesn't make viewers feel physically uncomfortable with recognition, reshoot it
2. "Would your hands be sweating?" (9:00) — this is the pivot. The moment the product stops being an indicator and becomes freedom from anxiety
3. "You're not broken" (11:30) — the emotional climax. If the viewer isn't slightly emotional here, the first 11 minutes didn't land
4. The last shot (someone walking away from their desk) — this is the aspirational image. Not lambos. Not profits. Just... peace. A person who placed a trade and went on with their life.

**Music:**
- 0:00-0:15: Heartbeat only. No music.
- 0:15-3:00: Very low drone. Tension. Think the opening of a horror movie — not scary, just... uneasy. Mirror the anxiety they're feeling.
- 3:00-4:30: Drone continues. Slight shift — the tension is becoming frustration.
- 4:30-6:30: Cleaner tone. The drone resolves slightly. You're moving from problem to understanding.
- 6:30-9:00: Music opens up. Still minimal, but there's warmth now. A pad. A gentle chord. The map has been found.
- 9:00-10:30: Subtle forward movement. Building.
- 10:30-11:30: Confident. Not triumphant — just steady. The walkthrough energy.
- 11:30-12:15: Music drops OUT. Complete silence behind the voice. "You're not broken" should land in quiet.
- 12:15-13:00: Gentle resolution. One note. One chord. Peace.
- 13:00-13:15: Silence, or the faintest possible tone. Then nothing.

**Ad cuts from this VSL:**

1. **"The Feeling" (0:00-0:45)** — Heartbeat, 3 AM phone, the sweating — cut to "You know this feeling." + CTA overlay. This is the top-of-funnel hook. Most people will never watch the full VSL but this clip will STOP the scroll.

5. **"Walk Away" (12:45-13:15)** — The closing shot. Someone leaving their desk. Position open. Not watching. 15-second cut for Stories/Reels.
