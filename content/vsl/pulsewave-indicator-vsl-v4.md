## PRODUCTION NOTES

**THE STRUCTURE — why this order:**

V3 opened with the pain. V4 opens with the PROOF. Here's why:

The $500 → $3,800 screenshot is the pattern interrupt. In a sea of "trading gurus" and indicators that promise the world, showing a real account with real gains in 20 days — including the red days — is the thing that stops the scroll and earns 45 seconds of attention.

But we don't explain HOW yet. We show the result, then say "I'll show you the indicator in a second — but first, you need to understand something." That creates an open loop. They KNOW there's a payoff coming (the indicator reveal), which pulls them through the emotional section that does the real selling.

By the time we reveal PulseWave at minute 7, they've:
1. Seen proof it works ($500 → $3,800)
2. Felt the pain of their own experience (sweaty hands, 3 AM, stopped out)
3. Been told the truth they've never heard ("it's not your psychology")
4. Been shown WHY they keep losing (entering in the middle)

So when the chart transforms with PulseWave, they don't think "oh, another indicator." They think "THAT'S how he did it."

Then at minute 11:30, we LOOP BACK to the proof. "Remember the $500? Here's what my entries looked like during those 20 days." Show the actual entries — all at levels. This closes the loop and makes the proof + the indicator inseparable in their mind.

**Recording the proof section (0:00-0:45):**

Mason — this needs to be YOUR screen recording. Raw. Unpolished. Show the real trading account.

What makes this section work:
- Show the actual balance. $500. Real.

- The final hold on $3,800 should last 3 full seconds. Let the number sit there.

The final shot is someone switching from their trading chart to Netflix while a position is open. This is the most powerful image in the entire VSL because it represents the OPPOSITE of what every trader experiences. Instead of staring at the chart with sweaty palms — they're watching a show. The trade is running in the background.

Not lambos. Not yachts. Not screenshots of profits.

Just a person who placed a trade and went on with their life.

That's the dream. That's what they're buying.
