## PRODUCTION NOTES

**THE CORE THESIS — memorize this before recording:**

Traders don't lose because of emotions. They lose because they enter trades with no structural reference points. Without knowing where support and resistance actually sit — across multiple timeframes, backed by real pivot data — every trade is a guess. PulseWave gives them the map. When you can see where the money is sitting, trading changes from gambling to navigation.

**Voice direction:** This is NOT a sales video. This is a conversation with a friend who's been through it and figured out the answer. No hype. No urgency tricks. No "but WAIT there's MORE." Calm. Measured. Slightly tired of the bullshit in trading education. The kind of person who's successful enough to not need to impress you.

**Pacing rules:**
- Every pause is intentional. When price reacts at a level, SHUT UP and let the chart speak. Three seconds minimum.
- Speed up slightly during the walkthrough section (showing how the layers work) — this is the "show me the features" section where momentum matters.
- Slow down during the problem section and the reframe. These are emotional. Give them space.
- The hook should feel almost uncomfortably slow. You're making them feel the silence of not knowing.

**Screen recording rules:**
- Dark TradingView theme ONLY
- NO other indicators loaded. PulseWave is the only thing on the chart.
- The "before" shots should feel genuinely chaotic — load 6 indicators and show the real mess
- The "after" shots should feel immediately calm. The visual contrast IS the argument.
- Record on BTC/USDT (Binance) 15-minute for the main walkthrough. This is what most viewers trade.
- Flash sections (other markets) use 5m or 15m timeframes for visual density

**Music:**
- First 3 minutes: almost nothing. A very low drone. The silence is the point.
- Minutes 3-5: subtle tension building. Low synth pad.
- Minutes 5-8 (the reveal + layers): slight energy increase. Still ambient. Think Trent Reznor scoring a documentary.
- Minutes 8-10 (walkthrough): slightly more movement. The viewer should feel momentum.
- CTA: music drops out completely. Just voice and silence.
- Final 30 seconds: single piano note or pad. Emotional, not dramatic.
