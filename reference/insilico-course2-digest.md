## Module 1: Trading Styles

### Four Styles
1. **Position Trading** — HTF macro; months/years; catch inflection points at macro tops/bottoms; ride with the trend
2. **Swing Trading** — Similar but smaller time horizon; daily/weekly/monthly swing points; not trying to catch macro tops/bottoms
3. **Day Trading** — Open and close within market hours; no overnight risk; crypto = 24/7
4. **Scalping** — Low TF; minutes/hours; best in **range-bound/consolidation** markets between S/R zones

### Types of Trades (Examples)
- **Position/Swing**: Identify HTF S/R flip → enter at support → stop below swing low → target at S/R breakdown level
- **Day Swing**: Daily chart → S/R flip entry → stop below swing low → TP at breakdown
- **Scalp**: Range-bound → buy demand zone → stop below swing low → exit at supply zone/range high
- Key acronyms: **DBS** (Demand Buyer Support), **SSR** (Seller Supply Resistance)

## Module 2: Defining Trade Setups

### LTE Methodology (Level → Trigger → Entry/Exit)
- **Level**: Clearly defined S/R zone (horizontal or diagonal)
- **Trigger**: Obvious price action reaction at the level (engulfing candles, hammers, shooting stars, dojis)
- **Entry/Exit**: Predefined order type after successful trigger
- **Critical rule**: "Triggers can be entries, but entries cannot be triggers"
- Analogy: **Ready** (level) → **Set** (trigger) → **Go** (entry)

### Entry Approaches
1. **Higher low from trigger** → market/limit order at higher low
2. **Higher high confirmation** → wait for minor resistance S/R flip, then enter on retest

### Trade Management
- **Bias**: Bullish or bearish view → forms trading thesis
- **First Barrier (FB)**: First minor S/R that might invalidate thesis; earliest warning sign
- **Invalidation = Stop Loss**: Predefined level where thesis is wrong → exit for a loss
- "Arbitrarily moving invalidation only leads to bruises on both ego and portfolio"
- Newer traders → **less involvement is better** (cut winners too early, hold losers too late)

### Exit Strategies
1. **Set and Forget (SNF)**: Predefine TP, place limit orders + stop loss, walk away. Best for newer traders gathering data.
2. **Trailing Stop Loss**: Move stop into profit at new S/R levels. Can be fixed dollar, fixed %, or S/R-based. Allows greater R multiples.
3. **Partial Take-Profits**: Reduce position by fixed % at predefined S/R levels. Balance booking profits vs. riding the trend.
- Example: $500 trailing stop turned a $1,200 TP into $2,800 gain on BTC

## Module 3: Price Action Concepts

### Candlestick Formations

**Two-candle:**
- **Bullish/Bearish Engulfing**: Second candle engulfs first in opposite direction. Must close past open+high/low of prior candle. Confirmed by higher volume on engulfing candle.
- **Tweezer Tops/Bottoms**: Two sessions with equal highs/lows. Usually hammers or shooting stars. Strong local top/bottom signal.

**Three-candle:**
- **Morning/Evening Star**: Strong candle → indecision (doji/spinning top) → reversal candle closing past midpoint of first. Evening star = bearish reversal at top. Morning star = bullish reversal at bottom.
- **Three Inside Up/Down** (Zoran's favorite trigger): Strong candle → second closes past midpoint → third closes past open+high/low of first. High hit rate for trend reversals at key S/R.
- **Three White Soldiers / Three Black Crows**: Three consecutive same-direction candles with strict rules — second body 2x first, third body 1.5x second, minimal counter-wicks. Most obvious reversal/continuation signal.

**Critical**: Only trade formations **after candle close** — never anticipate.

### Volume Analysis

**Four Volume Scenarios:**
| Price | Volume | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Rising | Rising | **Strong buying** (bullish) |
| Rising | Declining | **Weak buying** (possible reversal) |
| Declining | Rising | **Strong selling** (bearish) |
| Declining | Declining | **Weak selling** (possible reversal) |

- Volume = leading indicator for identifying which party is in control
- Volume confirms candlestick formations (engulfing on 2x volume = strong confirmation)
- Volume confirms breakouts/breakdowns at S/R levels
- Volume measures **liquidity** (important for advanced concepts)
- Works on ALL timeframes (weekly Tesla example, 4H ETH example)

### Classical Chart Patterns

**Key principles:**
- Pattern identification is **subjective** — stay objective
- Only trade **after pattern completion** (be reactive, not anticipatory)
- Volume should decline during formation, spike on breakout/breakdown
- Failed reversal pattern → price likely continues in original trend direction
- Target always = height of structure (pole) measured from breakout point

**Patterns:**
1. **Rising Wedge** (bearish): Higher lows outpace higher highs → breakdown. Target = pole height from breakdown. Wait for retest to enter short.
2. **Falling Wedge** (bullish): Lower lows outpace lower highs → breakout. Same target calculation. Sometimes goes to target without retest.
3. **Head & Shoulders** (bearish reversal): High → higher high → lower high. Neckline from shoulder bottoms. Target = head-to-neckline distance. Volume must decline from left shoulder and spike at right shoulder. **Failed H&S = bullish continuation.**
4. **Inverse H&S** (bullish reversal): Low → lower low → higher low. Same rules inverted.
5. **Descending Triangle**: Lower highs + equal lows. Can break either way → wait for confirmation.
6. **Ascending Triangle**: Higher lows + equal highs. Same principles.
7. **Symmetrical Triangle / Pennants**: Continuation. Target = pole height.
8. **Bull/Bear Flags** (continuation): Rectangular range in a trend. **Most important structures** — clear target (pole height), high probability, great R:R (examples showed 3.88:1 and 6.23:1 setups).

**Live examples:**
- Bear flag on EOS 12h: 3.88 R:R short setup with descending volume confirmation
- Bull flag on BTC 4h: 6.23 R:R long setup with S/R flip entry after indecision candle trigger
- Bullish pennant on BTC: $242 target hit exactly
- Descending triangle on BTC daily: 2.74 R:R short, took months to play out
- Ascending triangle on ETH daily: 2.84 R:R long to target

## Module 4: Trading Tools

### Fibonacci Retracements
- Derived from Fibonacci sequence (each number / next ≈ 0.618)
- Key levels: **50%** and **61.8%** (primary focus)
- Also: 23.6%, 38.2% (secondary)
- **Tool usage**: Measure from swing high to swing low
- **Acts as confluence** — Fib levels often align with S/R zones
- Not standalone; always combine with S/R identification first
- Example 1: Long at daily S/R flip → exit at 61.8% which coincided with S/R breakdown
- Example 2: Short at 61.8% retracement + S/R breakdown confluence
- Consistency and discipline are critical — journal your Fib-based trades and track hit rates

### Ichimoku Kinko Hyo ("Equilibrium at one glance")
- Developed over 30 years by Goichi Hosoda (released 1960s)
- **Trend identification system** — captures 70-80% of any given trend ("meat of the trend")
- **Does NOT work in range-bound markets** (many false signals)

**Five Components:**
1. **Tenkan-sen** (Conversion Line, light blue): Fast MA — avg of (high+low)/2 for short lookback
2. **Kijun-sen** (Baseline, yellow): **Most important component** — trend setter. Price above = bullish, below = bearish. Essentially a **dynamic 50% Fibonacci retracement**.
3. **Senkou Span A**: Avg of Tenkan + Kijun (plotted forward)
4. **Senkou Span B**: Avg of high+low for longer lookback (plotted forward)
5. **Chikou Span** (Lagging): Current price plotted backwards X periods. If inside price = range-bound.

**Kumo (Cloud)**: Area between Senkou Span A and B.
- SSA on top = bullish cloud. SSB on top = bearish cloud.
- **Thicker cloud = stronger S/R. Thinner = weaker.**
- **Kumo Twist**: When SSA and SSB cross (bullish or bearish)

**Settings:**
| Type | Conversion | Baseline | SSB | Displacement |
|------|-----------|----------|-----|-------------|
| Standard (TradFi) | 9 | 26 | 52 | 26 |
| Crypto | 10 | 30 | 60 | 30 |
| Smooth (doubled) | 20 | 60 | 120 | 30 |

Zoran prefers **smooth settings** — fewer signals, higher hit rate. No "right" setting — be consistent.

**Kumo Breakout Checklist (ALL 4 required):**
1. Price closes above/below the Kumo
2. Price closes above/below the Kijun-sen
3. Bullish/bearish Kumo twist present
4. Chikou span above/below price

**Entry Methods:**
- Market order once all 4 confirmed (less optimal)
- Limit order at Tenkan-sen (provides entry point)
- S/R flip after breakout (wait for retest)

**Kijun-sen as Trailing Stop:**
- Move stop loss below Kijun as trend progresses
- Kijun follows the trend dynamically (it IS a moving 50% retracement)
- Exit when price **closes** below/above Kijun (not just wicks — avoid fakeouts)
- Live example: ETH entry at 640, trailed Kijun from 603 → 670 → 707 → 860, exited ~890 after close below Kijun

**Key insight**: Ichimoku is great at entries but **not exits** — use trailing stops (Kijun), partial TPs, or other exit strategies.

### Oscillators & Divergences

**Oscillators:**
- Oscillate between upper (overbought) and lower (oversold) bounds
- Types: RSI, MACD, Stochastics, etc.
- Used for identifying exhaustion points, not standalone trade signals
- Just another point of confluence

**Regular Divergences** (signal trend REVERSAL):
- **Bullish**: Price makes lower low, oscillator makes higher low → seller exhaustion (found at bottom of trend)
- **Bearish**: Price makes higher high, oscillator makes lower high → buyer exhaustion (found at top of trend)

**Hidden Divergences** (signal trend CONTINUATION):
- **Bullish**: Price makes higher low, oscillator makes lower low → "buy the dip" opportunity
- **Bearish**: Price makes lower high, oscillator makes higher high → "sell the rally" opportunity

**Rules:**
- Connect tops to tops, bottoms to bottoms (be consistent)
- Div only plays out after crossing back from extreme to median
- Divs can build over multiple sessions
- Don't look for bearish continuation divs in an uptrend
- Never trade divergences alone — use as confluence only
- Divergences are **leading indicators** — not guarantees

## Module 5: Financial Instruments

### Derivatives
- **Definition**: Financial instruments deriving value from an underlying asset (stocks, bonds, currencies, commodities, crypto, indices)
- **Underlying asset** = spot/physical market
- **Uses**: Hedging risk (insurance) and speculation (betting on direction)
- Traded through **contracts** between 2+ parties

**Types:**
1. **Futures**: Specified price at specified date. Regulated exchange with clearing party. Used for hedging (wheat farmer example) and speculation.
2. **Forwards**: Same as futures but **not regulated** (OTC, business-to-business).
3. **Options**: Right (not obligation) to buy/sell at specified date (expiration) and price (strike). Less capital upfront. "Insurance policy" for portfolios.
4. **Swaps**: Exchange of cash flows between two parties. Interest rate swaps common in TradFi.
5. **Perpetual Swaps** (crypto-specific): No expiration date. Ongoing derivative used with leverage/margin. Most liquid instrument on crypto exchanges.
